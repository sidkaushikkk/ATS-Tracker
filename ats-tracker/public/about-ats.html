What is an Applicant Tracking System (ATS)?

Understanding how ATS works is the first step toward building a resume that reaches recruiters instead of being filtered out.

Introduction

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is recruitment software used by companies to automate hiring. Instead of recruiters manually reviewing every application, ATS scans, organizes, and ranks resumes based on job requirements.

Today, more than 90% of medium and large organizations use ATS to manage thousands of applications for every job opening. If your resume isn't ATS-friendly, it may never reach a human recruiter.

How ATS Works

1

Resume Submission

Candidates upload resumes through company career portals or job boards.

2

Resume Parsing

The ATS extracts information such as name, education, experience, projects, skills, certifications, and contact details.

3

Keyword Matching

It compares your resume against keywords mentioned in the job description.

4

Scoring

Your resume receives a relevance score depending on skills, experience, formatting, and keyword match.

5

Recruiter Review

Recruiters review the highest-ranked resumes before scheduling interviews.

Why ATS Rejects Resumes

  • Missing important keywords from the job description.
  • Using tables, graphics, icons, or images that ATS cannot parse.
  • Complex formatting and multiple columns.
  • Incorrect section headings.
  • Unsupported file formats.
  • Poor readability and inconsistent fonts.
  • Insufficient experience matching the role.

Characteristics of an ATS-Friendly Resume

Simple Layout

Use a clean single-column design without unnecessary graphics.

Relevant Keywords

Include skills and terminology found in the job description naturally.

Standard Headings

Use headings like Education, Experience, Projects, Skills, and Certifications.

Readable Fonts

Use fonts such as Arial, Calibri, Helvetica, or Times New Roman.

Correct File Format

Submit resumes as PDF or DOCX when requested by employers.

Quantified Achievements

Use measurable accomplishments instead of generic responsibilities.

Common ATS Myths

Myth Reality
ATS automatically rejects resumes. ATS ranks and organizes resumes; recruiters still make hiring decisions.
Adding hundreds of keywords guarantees selection. Keyword stuffing often lowers resume quality and recruiter confidence.
Fancy designs improve hiring chances. Many ATS systems struggle with graphics, icons, and complex layouts.
ATS only checks keywords. Modern systems also evaluate experience, job titles, education, and overall relevance.

Best Practices

  • Customize your resume for every job application.
  • Mirror important terminology from the job description.
  • Highlight measurable achievements.
  • Keep formatting clean and professional.
  • Use bullet points instead of long paragraphs.
  • Proofread carefully before submission.
  • Ensure contact details are clearly visible.
